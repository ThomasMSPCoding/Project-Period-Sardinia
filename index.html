Sardinia the Island

Welcome to Sardinia Science Center!


Here we aim to provide a platform for scientists and tourists alike to discover what the island of Sardinia has to offer in terms of science.

Sardinia is very well known for its culture and beauty, but did you know that it was once inhabited by the ancient shark Megalodon? It was also once populated by the very intriguing Nuragic civilization, who left behind amazing structural remains that can still be found on the island. Sardinia houses a vast amount of important paleontological and archaeological sites waiting to be explored, as well as museums displaying the artefacts found in these sites such as the National Archaeological Museum of Cagliari.

In terms of nature you’ll be no less disappointed, Sardinia has plant and animal species specific to the island, they aren’t found anywhere else! The Giara horses, one of the few species of wild horses left in Europe, and the spiny cornflower, a plant as weird as it is beautiful, are just two of the many plants and animals to be discovered in Sardinia.
