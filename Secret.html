Secret Hidden Spots

Escape the ordinary and uncover Sardinia like never before. Beyond its famous beaches and bustling resorts lies a world of hidden coves, ancient ruins, and untouched natural beauty that only the most curious explorers ever find. From secluded beaches and secret canyons to mysterious ancient villages, Sardinia’s off-the-beaten-path wonders are waiting to be discovered.

Whether you’re seeking tranquil escapes, thrilling hikes, or a journey through history, these secret spots will make your trip to Sardinia unforgettable. Dive into our guide to explore the island’s best-kept secret places the locals love and few tourists know about.

Bosa and its Castle

It is well worth the trip to this small village! With its multicolored homes perched atop a hill and topped by a castle, Bosa is a very attractive city. The town offers breathtaking sunset views and is where the Temo River meets the sea. But don't forget to explore the city and meander through its winding streets! It is situated in Sardinia's west. Don't overlook the unique yellow wine known as "Malvasia."

Figure 1.Town of Bosa (Yepsea (2024))

The Bosa Castle, perched atop a hill overlooking the Temo River, dates back to the 11th century, with most structures built in the 13th century by the Arborea dynasty. It played a strategic role in medieval Sardinia, defending against invasions during the giudicati period and later under Pisan and Catalan rule. Over centuries, it underwent modifications, suffered damages, and was eventually abandoned. Today, after significant restoration, it stands as a cultural and historical treasure, featuring well-preserved medieval walls, defensive towers, frescoes, and panoramic views of Bosa and its surroundings. A visit offers a glimpse into Sardinia's rich medieval past.

Figure 2.Bosa castle (Yepsea (2024) )

Torre di Porto Giunco

Check out Torre di Porto Giunco if you're looking for a nice place to see a sunset or dawn. There are stunning, unhindered 360-degree panorama views of the Carbonara Cape from this vantage point. An ancient Spanish tower, constructed of 16th-century stone and in poor condition, stands at the summit. Although it is inaccessible, its location allows for fantastic views of the surrounding coastline. A short route takes you up in 20 to 30 minutes if you park your car near the Porto Giunco spiaggia. Enjoy the sunset here with some nibbles and a nice bottle of wine.

Figure 3.Torre di Porto Giunco Torre di Porto Giunco Views (Admin, 2023)

Cala Coticcio ("Tahiti") - Caprera

Nestled on the stunning island of Caprera, part of the La Maddalena Archipelago in northeastern Sardinia, Cala Coticcio is a hidden paradise often referred to as "Tahiti" for its breathtaking turquoise waters and powdery white sand.Surrounded by smooth granite rocks and Mediterranean vegetation, this beach offers a unique blend of natural beauty and tranquility. Its crystal-clear waters are perfect for snorkeling, revealing a vibrant underwater world of marine life.

Due to its location within the protected La Maddalena National Park, access to Cala Coticcio is regulated to preserve its pristine environment. Visitors can reach the beach via a scenic guided hike through Caprera’s rugged landscapes, making the journey as memorable as the destination. For those seeking a serene escape, Cala Coticcio promises an unforgettable experience in one of Sardinia’s most enchanting settings.

Figure 4.Cala Coticcio, Isola di Caprera - La Maddalena. Credits: Ellisa Locci.

Gorropu Canyon - Supramonte

Gorropu Canyon is one of Europe’s deepest and most spectacular gorges, a true natural wonder in Sardinia. Carved by the Flumineddu River over millennia, the canyon boasts towering limestone walls that rise over 500 meters, creating a dramatic and awe-inspiring landscape.

Accessible via scenic hiking trails, the journey to Gorropu Canyon is an adventure in itself, passing through lush forests and rocky terrain. The canyon is a haven for nature lovers, offering rare flora, crystal-clear streams, and the chance to spot Sardinian wildlife. It’s also a favorite destination for climbers, with challenging routes along its sheer cliffs.

Whether you’re exploring its mystical depths or marveling at its panoramic views from above, Gorropu Canyon offers an unforgettable escape into Sardinia’s wild and untouched beauty.

Figure 5. Gorropu Canyon (Gorropu - Sito Ufficiale, 2019)

Tiscali Village - Supramonte

Perched within a collapsed cave in the rugged Supramonte range, Tiscali Village is a mysterious Nuragic settlement that dates back thousands of years. Shrouded in myth and history, this archaeological site offers a glimpse into Sardinia’s ancient past, surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty.

Accessible only by a challenging hike through steep trails and forested paths, the journey to Tiscali is as rewarding as the destination. Upon arrival, visitors are greeted by the remains of stone dwellings built into the cave’s walls, blending seamlessly with the limestone surroundings. The site’s elevated position also offers panoramic views of the Lanaittu Valley, enhancing its mystical allure.

A visit to Tiscali Village is a unique opportunity to step back in time, uncover Sardinia’s prehistoric heritage, and immerse yourself in the serene beauty of its mountainous landscapes.

Figure 6.(Virtual Archaeology - The Village Of Tiscali: An Important Archaeological And Natural Context, s. f.)

Piscine di Monte Ferru - Barbagia

Hidden in the unspoiled landscapes of Barbagia, Piscine di Monte Ferru is a serene collection of natural pools and cascading waterfalls, surrounded by lush vegetation and rugged granite formations. This tranquil retreat offers a perfect escape for nature enthusiasts seeking a peaceful and refreshing experience.

The pools, formed by crystal-clear streams flowing through the Monte Ferru hills, provide an ideal spot for swimming or simply relaxing in harmony with nature. The area is also home to a variety of flora and fauna, making it a haven for photographers and wildlife lovers alike.

Accessible via scenic trails, Piscine di Monte Ferru invites visitors to immerse themselves in the untouched beauty of Sardinia’s interior, far from the bustling tourist spots. Whether you’re hiking, picnicking, or taking a dip in the cool waters, this hidden gem promises an unforgettable connection to the island’s wild heart.
